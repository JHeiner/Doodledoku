Playing sudoku online is fun, but it can get a little annoying because each site has a slightly different way to interact. There is at least some agreement on how to solve a cell: clicking in it then typing a number almost always works. Almost. But when it comes to marking candidates there is a lot of variation. The goal of this extension is to let me work on puzzles the way I want, regardless of what site they come from.

A secondary goal is to keep the extension as small as possible: there is no need to build yet another tool that does absolutely everything. The inspiration comes from the observation that the interaction with any puzzle becomes exactly the same once it is printed out. So the basic job of the extension is to act like the web page is a piece of paper and let the user doodle on it.

Basics:
  • Click and drag to doodle.
  • Click on an empty spot to put a dot.
  • Click on existing doodles to erase.

There is one advanced feature: holding "Ctrl" and erasing gets rid of the doodle clicked on and all the doodles that were drawn afterwards.
